Model Context

The baseline uses each team's prior 20 regular-season franchise games before tipoff. Features are recent points for, points against, scoring margin, and win percentage for the listed home team versus the away team.

Historical backtesting put this baseline at 0.6232 log loss versus 0.6931 for random 50/50, about a 10.1% relative improvement. Picks below 55% are marked as coin flips because prior diagnostics showed that low-confidence buckets are noisy.

When a market file is supplied, value side is calculated as model win probability minus the no-vig market-implied win probability. Listed spreads are market context only; this is not yet a spread-cover model.

ELI5Think of the model as a scorekeeper with a short memory. It looks at how each team has played recently, especially scoring margin and win rate, then converts that into a win chance. It does not know who is hurt or resting.
This model does not train on injuries, player availability, betting markets, travel distance, or lineup strength. Optional market inputs are used only after prediction to compare model probability against market-implied probability. Expansion teams have shorter WNBA histories, so their rolling windows may contain fewer than 20 games.
